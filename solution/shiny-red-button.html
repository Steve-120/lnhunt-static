Solution: Shiny Red Button
Answer: BUZZER BEATER

Written by lightbulbMEOW

Level Link Solution
1 level1.htm Press the red button.
2 level2.htm Press the green button.
3 level3.htm Scroll down and press the yellow button.
4 level4.htm Press the invisible button on the right.
5 level5.htm Press the 1-pixel button. You may zoom in to make this easier.
6 level6.htm In some browsers, hovering over the button shows a preview of the link it will send you. The button on row 4 column 5 sends you to the next level.
7 level7.htm Resize the window to reshape the text, then press the button behind the text.
8 level8.htm Press Tab to select the button outside, then press Enter.
9 level9.htm Press Tab twice to select the button behind, then press Enter.
10 level10.htm Enter the URL on the button.
11 level-11.htm Right-click on the broken image then open it in a new tab.
12 level-twelve.htm The title says “thirteenth-level.htm”
13 thirteenth-level.htm Select the hidden text to reveal “thenextlevel.htm”
14 thenextlevel.htm Highlight the black text to reveal the message. It instructs us to find letters that are a very very dark shade of gray (#010101). Using a tool like MS Paint can help reveal these letters. The answer is “enlighten.htm”
15 enlighten.htm Remove “light” from the URL to get “enen.htm”
16 enen.htm Reading down the diagonal gives “diagonal.htm”
17 diagonal.htm Highlighting the entire page gives “blue.htm”
18 blue.htm These songs are the lyrics to Blue (Da Ba Dee) by Eiffel 65. Taking the missing words and their first letters gives “adjacent”
19 adjacent.htm Translating this from morse code gives “gyroscope”
20 gyroscope.htm Visiting these links teleports us to certain words. These words are “THE ANSWER IS TAXONOMIC RANK BETWEEN DOMAIN AND PHYLUM”, which is “KINGDOM”
21 kingdom.htm Identify the 7 countries. Notice that all these countries are 7-letter words. Diagonalizing these country names gives “COUNTRY”
22 country.htm Treat the countries as dots. Connecting between dots on the map reveals the answer to be “TRAVEL”
23 travel.htm Hovering over the buttons, some of them are selectable while some are not. Translating this using braille gives “GOBACK”
24 goback.htm Press the back button.
25 gobackevenfurther.htm What comes before level1.htm is level0.htm
0 level0.htm This is a metapuzzle. Placing the webpage links on the blanks and extracting the highlighted letters gives “welcometolevelnegativeone”
-1 welcometolevelnegativeone.htm Change “one” to “two” in the URL
-2 welcometolevelnegativetwo.htm Notice that pressing left button sends you to “welcomelevelnegativeone.htm”, NOT “welcometolevelnegativeone.htm” This tells us that we actually need to visit “welcomelevelnegativetwo.htm” instead, and scrolling down reveals a button to Level -3.
-3 deceptivebuttons.htm The button to the right is not invisible, it is just white. Opening it in a new tab on a darker background reveals “whitewashed.htm”
-4 whitewashed.htm Opening the 1-pixel button in a new tab reveals that it’s actually a 3000x3000 image. Reading the tiny letters in the corners give “micrometer”.
-5 micrometer.htm The buttons resemble a periodic table. Press the button which corresponds to the element Lead, which is element 82.
-6 element82.htm Convert the number of “blah”s using A1Z26 cipher to get “bilingual”
-7 bilingual.htm Up until now, all the buttons in the negative levels are named “der_button.png”, “neerg_button.png”, etc. These are color names in reverse. To get the answer to this level, rename the button in this level to “kcalb_button.png”
-8 imblackthenimwhite.htm Type out the lyrics of the song into the URL (e.g., whatthehellsgoingon.htm) to get “FIRST LINE OF PRECHORUS PART OF SONG”. This is referring to the song ECHO by Crusher-P.
-9 whycantisee.htm The embossed letters read “FACELESS”
-10 faceless.htm Solve the sudoku puzzle to get “MANHATTAN DOT HTM”
-11 manhattan.htm Use a color picker tool to get the hex values of the buttons, then search up their names. The Wikipedia page for “Shades of Blue” is useful here.
-12 bluebirds.htm Count the number of words between the two words using the dictionary from gyroscope.htm (for example, WORD and WORDPLAY are 15 words apart), then convert the numbers using A1Z26 cipher.
-13 oceanic.htm Converting the flags to letters gives “MR WORLDWIDE OTHER NAME”, which is “Pitbull”
-14 pitbull.htm Press Tab to reveal the text “EMPATHY”
-15 empathy.htm Go to Level i, which is an imaginary number.
i leveli.htm Each venn diagram consists of two URL pages. The letters in the middle show their common letters, with a letter missing. For example, in the first one the two pages are “micrometer.htm” and “kingdom.htm”. The missing common letter between them is O. Repeating this for all the others gives OPPOSITE OF LIGHT, which can be either “dark” or “heavy”
±1+i dark.htm / heavy.htm Combine the three pieces to get “darkening.htm” or “heavyweight.htm”
±2+i darkening.htm / heavyweight.htm We’re presented with an image of Doraemon and Pikachu. Viewing the image on a different background reveals hidden text, “evolved form” and “preteen boy’s name”. These are “raichu” and “nobita” respectively.
±3+i nobita.htm / raichu.htm Overlap the two images to reveal a semaphore or pigpen cipher message in the holes.
±4+i pigstep.htm / flagship.htm For pigstep.htm, use the shifts from flagship.htm to get “DIGITAL”, and vice versa to get “QUANTITY”
±5+i digital.htm / quantity.htm Use morse code in two different ways to get “STAYS” or “UNTIED”
±6+i stays.htm / untied.htm XOR the two images to reveal a map showing that we should go to Level -i
-i level-i.htm First, use the answers from the previous levels, then place the corresponding letters on the pieces. Then, use the same method of connecting the dots from Level 22 to spell out a word. The white pieces spell out “BUZZER” while the black pieces spell out “BEATER”
buzzer.htm / beater.htm Combine the two answers together
buzzerbeater.htm Done! Go back to the puzzle page and submit BUZZER BEATER

Author’s Notes

This puzzle is inspired by all those URL-changing riddle games from the 2000s, like Notpron, Amnesya, and Weffriddles. A much more recent riddle game, Polychromasia, is what finally pushed me to make my own.
