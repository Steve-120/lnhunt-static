Solution: Obligatory Balatro Puzzle
Answer: DAVY JONES

Written by kt3, lightbulbmeow, steve120; Ideated with Cauchy, juckter

We are presented with some “Balatro” boards. In the original Balatro, the playing cards and the jokers in play affect how the round is scored, with the jokers having a wide variety of effects. Scoring is done first on the playing cards from left to right alongside jokers with card-specific effects, and then the jokers that affect the final score are activated. Here, we try to figure out how this variation of “Balatro” is scored.

We can use the first boards with given round scores as examples, and make some informed guesses on how the game works and how jokers work and calculate the round score for the latter boards. It is intended that there might be ambiguities on how certain jokers work; they are later pinned down once we make our final calculations.

Rules

Unlike in Balatro, cards are multiplied together in this puzzle, and exponentiated by their suit (D=1, C=2, H=3, S=4). The jokers have the following effects:

Joker Activates on Effect
2∑^ Final round score Let x be the sum of exponents of the score’s prime factorization. Replace the exponent of 2 in the final score with x.
Pyramid All playing cards Score each playing card value n with the nth triangular number (given by n(n+1)/2) instead.
Primorial Leftmost playing card Score the leftmost playing card value n with n# (the primorial of n) instead.
Venn diagram Leftmost playing card Score the leftmost playing card value n with LCM(1,2,...,n) instead.
Duplicate Rightmost card Create a duplicate of the rightmost card.
Swap Rightmost playing card Create a duplicate of the rightmost playing card, with the last two digits swapped.
Sigma Final round score Convert the final score x to σ(x) (the sum of positive divisors of x).

The stone card has a fixed value of 2025 and is not considered a playing card, so it is unaffected by the pyramid joker in particular. Playing cards with a black color are negative cards that divide the round score by its value rather than multiply.

To extract, we could Google the numbers and find out that these are the orders of the sporadic groups. In fact, the flavortext clues the monster group, the largest of the sporadic groups. There are 26 sporadic groups, so we use A1Z26 to extract, indexed by size.
