Solution: Erm, Actually
Answer: RATTLESNAKE

Written by Cauchy, Idontknow, juckter, kt3, adfun12345

We are presented with statements and a lot of question marks. As the title suggests, these statements are not exactly accurate, and we can pedantically correct them.

This puzzle is a parody of the Dropout show Um, Actually!, where contestants are asked to correct the given prompts by preceding their corrections with the phrase “Um, actually”. For this puzzle, we use “Erm, actually”, and this phrase matches the first question marks of each prompt, meaning we are expected to fill in enough of the intended correction to extract the letter that would fit in the underscore. The words were chosen to be important to the correction and so that it should be possible to determine what the missing letter is without necessarily being sure of the entire rest of the sentence. Reading the missing letters in the provided prompt order gives “ANS RATTLESNAKE”, meaning the final answer is just RATTLESNAKE.

In order, the prompts and their corrections are (with the relevant corrections bolded):

In the video game Celeste, you control Celeste as she climbs to the top of a mountain, meeting other characters such as Theo and Mr. Oshiro along the way.
Erm, actually, you control Madeline. Celeste is the name of the mountain.

Stokes' theorem is an important theorem in calculus that relates line integrals to surface integrals. However, it is also a classic example of Stigler's law of eponymy, as George Stokes never got to prove the theorem, but he is still credited for its name for being the first to state it.
Erm, actually, Lord Kelvin was the first to state the theorem.

In Galaxy Quest, Tim Allen plays Peter Quincy Taggart, the has-been star actor of the titular Star Trek-like TV show. When he's invited by real aliens to captain a working replica of the show's ship to save their species from extinction, he has to step up and work together with his former colleagues, played by actors including Alan Rickman, Sigourney Weaver, and Sam Rockwell.
Erm, actually, Tim Allen plays Jason Nesmith. Peter Quincy Taggart is the name of the character played by Nesmith in the in-universe Galaxy Quest TV show.
