Solution: Flex Tape
Answer: UNGLUED

Written by kt3, juckter

We start by solving the Rows Garden puzzle:

Amidst the regular clues, there are four that point us towards the next step:

Clue Answer
First name of the designer of a relevant two-page infographic REBECCA
Last name of the designer of a relevant two-page infographic CELSI
Elements important to this puzzle HUES
Something you will need to do to solve this puzzle UNFOLD

Searching for Rebecca Celsi leads us to a two-page infographic describing the process to create a dodecahexaflexagon. (https://www.rebeccacelsi.com/graphic-design?pgid=ktjc076o-24001c7b-9856-4f5b-9657-6100833ab346, https://www.rebeccacelsi.com/graphic-design?pgid=ktjc076o-f3ba6e6b-0479-49ae-a6fe-dfc16ed45c54)

Each of the twelve faces in the dodecahexaflexagon is given a specific color in the infographic. Looking back at our bloom clues, we see that each of our twelve colors appears in exactly one of the clues

By using these colors, we can associate each face of the flexagon with a specific Bloom in the Rows Garden. Our next step is to write the answers to each bloom on the corresponding faces. In order to disambiguate between multiple possible placements, we look at the two diagrams we haven’t used:

  • The upper diagram shows a face of the flexagon with glue on the top triangle. This tells us that we should rotate the faces such that the triangles we glued to assemble the flexagon are always at the top.
  • The lower diagram corresponds to the map in Page 2 of the infographic, and highlights all the states where Red appears opposite another face. This diagram indicates that whenever a color appears as the front face in multiple states, we should always take the one that is closest to the center in the map.

This gives us an unambiguous way to label each triangle of the flexagon by writing down the answer of the corresponding bloom in the identified state and orientation. As suggested by the last of our special clues, the last step is to UNFOLD the flexagon, returning it to its original state as a strip.

Looking at the middle of our strip, spanning both rows, we obtain the phrase STP THIRD SONG FOR LETTERMAN. This refers to the band Stone Temple Pilots’ third performance on David Letterman’s late night show. The song in question is UNGLUED, the answer to this puzzle and an accurate description of our trusty flexagon at this stage.
