Written by kt3, Cauchy, Idontknow, juckter, lightbulbmeow, Rio Futaba

We are presented with 9 grids that clue each row and each column, similar to popular games like Pokedoku. Each grid also comes with a given sorting mechanism. Finally, an Extractiondoku is given at the end, instructing us to sort our grids by title lengths, although that is a bit curious because many of our grid titles are of length 8.

After trying to fill in the grids, we can figure out that each grid resolves to a unique solution. However, the sorting mechanism is particularly tricky, resulting in many clashes among the entries (e.g. many of the items in Tacticku released together in the same patch).

The main aha for this puzzle is that the clashes are intentional, and they may be interpreted as pencil markings to a Sudoku that can be resolved with logic. The arrangement of the grids at the end also features some ambiguity, but ultimately resolves to the following unique grid.

Using the given equation for each row, we obtain numbers that spell out ZONE BLOCK in A1Z26.
