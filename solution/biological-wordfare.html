Solution: Biological Wordfare
Answer: CHOCOMINT

Written by juckter

We are presented with a series of long, seemingly gibberish strings of text, and a corresponding amount of blanks at the bottom with numbers from 1 to 8. Looking at them carefully enough, we realize that each string is in fact a combination of the scientific names for exactly two biological organisms, with possibly some modifications.

Each pair of names clues a phrase that is obtained by combining the common names of these organisms in a way similar to the corresponding string. To aid with identification, the strings are provided in alphabetical order of the corresponding phrase.

We now turn our attention to the blanks below, each of them fits one of our obtained phrases. Extracting the letters in each of the squares labeled 1 gives the message NOW USE TAXONOMIC RANKS. As it turns out, each scientific name in the above phrases points not only to a certain organism, but to a specific taxon of organisms.

After identifying the correct taxon for each scientific name, the last observation is that each pair of combined names has one taxonomic rank that lies exactly in-between those of the combined pair. This is also clued by the flavor text, which mentions compromising opposite points of view. Using this middle rank, we can extract the letter corresponding to this rank in each clue (here we start at Domain = 1, and go all the way up to Species = 8).

Reading the extracted letters in the order of the blanks we get THEOBROMA MENTHA NINE. Here, the nine is an enumeration, Theobroma is the genus containing the cacao tree, and Mentha is the genus containing mint plants. Following the puzzle’s logic, we combine the common names of these to obtain the final answer CHOCOMINT.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle came to be as I was reading about the Holotypic Occlupanid Research Group (https://www.horg.com/horg/). I thought the concept of giving scientific names to non-organisms was very funny, and thought about ways it could be done. Eventually I came to the concept of creating a scientific name for a Peanut Butter and Jelly sandwich by combining those of Peanuts and Jellies, and from there the puzzle came together fairly quickly.

Since the construction was fairly unconstrained, a lot of the clues that I ultimately decided to go with were just ones that allowed me to show off cool organisms. I love learning about interesting plants and animals, and although the actual puzzle only concerns itself with their nomenclature, I decided I needed to include some cool ones! My favorite clue in terms of the organisms I got to include was STICK IN THE MUD, stick bugs and mudskippers are both awesome :)

The answer for this puzzle was slightly changed from what was drafted for the meta originally, since I realized I could make the final extraction give a “do it again” step instead. I hope people weren’t too tripped up by the somewhat unusual term for the combination! Throughout working on this puzzle, I was constantly thinking about a relevant song by GYARI about chocomint flavored things 絶対にチョコミントを食べるアオイチャン. As luck would have it, over two years later this would become an unintentional reference to a second japanese song!
