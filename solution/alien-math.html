Solution: Alien Math
Answer: CHEBYSHEV INTEGRAL

Written by kt3, lightbulbMEOW; Peer-reviewed by Cauchy

The puzzle starts out with a section described as a "O", initially showing some more fundamental-looking equations and building up to more complicated configurations.

A good first guess is that the double connection with a line on the top curve shows equality. For example, it says that square equals square. It also seems to show that some identity elements of operations, namely O for the double connection with no lines, and φ for the self-connecting curve.

However, solvers may realize that the double connection with a line on the top curve is not an equals sign, but the subtraction operator, and the O section is describing different expressions that evaluate to 0.

In this puzzle, circle-like objects represent numbers, while squares represent variables. Operations are denoted by curve lines. See the diagram below.

Nubmers are represented in base-5. The digits are read from largest circle to smallest circle.

Once we have enough understanding, we can solve the ⦻ (non-zero) part of the puzzle. The evaluations A1Z26 spell out ROTATENINETYDEGCW, which tells us to resolve everything but rotated 90 degrees clockwise.

Thankfully, we can reuse most of our work, as this rotation only swaps out 1 and 3, as well as 2 and 4, per digit in the base 5 representation of each number. Redoing the calculations gives us CHEBYSHEV INTEGRAL, the answer to this puzzle.
