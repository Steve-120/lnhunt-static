Solution: May I Have A Silly Crossword?
Answer: DOUGHNUT

Written by kt3

Solvers are given four pairs of across and down clues and four independent criss-cross grids. The clues solve as follows:

Across, in order

Blue dye mineral (starting word) (1st)LAPIS
Water starter from Sinnoh (Pokemon) (3rd)PIPLUP
Common animal with eight legs (8th)SPIDER
Nintendo species living in Onions (2nd)PIKMIN

Down, in order

Minecraft mob that covets gold (1st)PIGLIN
Scottish guy with numerous bones? (1st)NAPIER
Blank Games, Fortnite company (3rd)EPIC
Twitch meme; Pepe with "oxygen" (6th)COPIUM

After entering these words into the criss-crosses, the intersections (check the cross!) read PIEM.

The first key insight is that PI is present in all clue answers. The second is that PIEM is a name for certain mnemonics for the digits of pi (the title also alluding to a famous one, with the flavortext extending the theme).

This inspires us to check the number of letters of each clue and find this number string in pi (using, say, https://www.angio.net/pi/), with the parenthetical saying the certain nth occurrence. This allows us to map each answer to a number, the position where such occurrence starts.

The final insight is that this position is a number whose number of digits matches the number of letters of each clue answer. Extracting the digits corresponding to PI, taking these A1Z26, and reading in grid order gives us the answer DOUGHNUT.
