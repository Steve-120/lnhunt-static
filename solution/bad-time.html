Solution: Bad Time
Answer: UNDERDOG

Written by kt3, lightbulbMEOW

We are presented with a series of audio clips and a list of cryptic clues and cryptics that don't seem to be solvable.

This puzzle is a reference to the Megalovania memes on YouTube where the song is being transformed in some way, like beats being swapped.

The audio clips show the beginning of Megalovania but transformed in some way. The first step of the puzzle is to identify these transformations.

Audio clips Audio Transformation Word Transformation
Audio clip 1 Remove first note Remove first letter
Audio clip 2 Play first five notes after last five notes Swap word halves
Audio clip 3 Remove middle two notes Remove middle bigram
Audio clip 4 Prepend a G note Prepend a letter G
Audio clip 5 Append a D note Append a letter D
Audio clip 6 Shuffle the notes, with exactly one fixed note Anagram the letters, with exactly one fixed letter

These interpretations are consistent with the footnotes. There are 11 each MIDI-playable Gs and Ds, and the exact number of note shuffles with one fixed point of the first ten notes of Megalovania is 41124, accounting for multiplicity (recognizing this is not that important).

The next step is to notice that some of the cryptic clues seem almost solvable if some word was altered somehow. An intended break-in is the following example:

"Even mage armies are huge (4)"

MEGA seems like a good candidate for this clue, with definition "huge".

If the first letter from "mage" was removed, the clue becomes

"Even age armies are huge (4)"

The wordplay part resolves to _G_A_M_E_ (take even letters of "age armies"). If we swap the halves of the word GAME, we get MEGA (!!!). This is the answer to the cryptic clue.

It turns out, all the clues follow this pattern: One of the words in the clue should be transformed to get a valid wordplay, and the answer from the wordplay should also be transformed to match the definition part.
